## What is Ketamine?
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic originally developed in the 1960s. It's primarily used in medical settings for its rapid-acting sedative effects. Unlike traditional anesthetics, ketamine preserves breathing and heart function, making it valuable in emergencies.

In recent years, ketamine has gained attention for off-label uses, such as treating depression and chronic pain. Research from institutions like the FDA and WHO highlights its potential in mental health therapy, especially for treatment-resistant depression.

### Medical Applications of Ketamine
- **Anesthesia**: Commonly used in surgeries, veterinary medicine, and trauma care.
- **Mental Health Treatment**: Low-dose infusions show promise for PTSD, anxiety, and suicidal ideation.
- **Pain Management**: Effective for neuropathic pain and complex regional pain syndrome.

By 2026, ketamine clinics have expanded globally, offering supervised sessions with esketamine (a nasal spray variant) approved for depression.
